# ☕ Sip & Speak

# Teaching Methodology

Version: v0.1.0

---

# Our Philosophy

Language is not learned by memorizing.

Language is learned by using.

Sip & Speak is built around communication first.

Grammar and vocabulary exist only to support meaningful conversations.

---

# The Learning Cycle

Every session follows the same cycle.

1.

Speak.

↓

2.

Notice what language is missing.

↓

3.

Discover useful language.

↓

4.

Use it immediately.

↓

5.

Reflect.

---

# Lexical Approach

Sip & Speak teaches language through lexical chunks.

Never isolated words.

Example:

❌ improve

✅ improve your chances

❌ nervous

✅ get nervous around strangers

❌ relationship

✅ build meaningful relationships

---

# Vocabulary Policy

We don't teach vocabulary.

We teach usable language.

Every new expression must:

• sound natural

• be common in educated spoken English

• be useful in real conversations

• appear multiple times during the session

---

# Translation Policy

No direct translation.

Instead we use:

• simple English definitions

• examples

• context

• immediate speaking practice

Thinking in English is one of the project's goals.

---

# Grammar Policy

Grammar is never taught explicitly.

Instead, grammar appears naturally through useful expressions.

Learners notice patterns while speaking.

---

# Video Policy

Every video must:

• be authentic

• sound natural

• be engaging

• be under 6 minutes

Videos exist to inspire conversations—not to test listening.

---

# Speaking Time

Target:

Learners should spend at least

70%

of the session speaking.

Teacher explanations (or reading instructions) should be minimal.

---

# Question Design

Questions should never have obvious answers.

Good questions create stories.

Bad questions create one-word answers.

Instead of:

"Do you like travelling?"

Ask:

"What's the biggest misconception tourists have about your country?"

---

# Activities We Love

• storytelling

• ranking

• debates

• roleplays

• dilemma discussions

• hot takes

• guessing games

• speed speaking

• explain without saying

• this or that

• impossible choices

---

# Activities We Avoid

✗ fill in the gaps

✗ matching

✗ translation

✗ multiple choice

✗ copying

✗ mechanical repetition

---

# Success

A successful session is not the one where learners remember every expression.

A successful session is the one where learners genuinely forget they're speaking English.

---

# Method Rule #1

Conversation creates learning.

Not the other way around.

---

# Method Rule #2

Useful language always beats difficult language.

---

# Method Rule #3

If an activity doesn't make learners speak, rethink it.
