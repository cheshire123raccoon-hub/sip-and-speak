# ☕ Sip & Speak

# Design Language

Version: v0.1.0

---

# Design Philosophy

Sip & Speak is designed to disappear.

The interface should never compete with conversations.

Instead, it quietly supports them.

Every visual decision should reduce friction and increase focus.

---

# Visual Identity

Sip & Speak feels like:

• a modern café

• a beautifully designed notebook

• a premium productivity app

• a calm creative space

Not a classroom.

Not an online course.

Not a game.

---

# Keywords

Elegant

Minimal

Warm

Calm

Premium

Modern

Human

---

# Color Philosophy

Colors should create comfort rather than excitement.

Primary accents are used sparingly.

Whitespace is part of the design.

The interface breathes.

---

# Typography

Readable before decorative.

Hierarchy over decoration.

Large headings.

Comfortable paragraph spacing.

Excellent readability on laptops.

---

# Layout

Everything lives inside comfortable containers.

No edge-to-edge content.

Generous spacing.

Large margins.

Consistent rhythm.

---

# Cards

Cards are the foundation of the interface.

They should feel light.

Rounded.

Floating slightly above the background.

Never heavy.

Never cluttered.

---

# Buttons

Buttons should feel effortless.

Primary actions attract attention.

Secondary actions remain quiet.

Only one primary action should exist on each screen.

---

# Motion

Animation exists to explain movement.

Never to impress.

Fast.

Smooth.

Natural.

Every transition should have a purpose.

---

# Progress

Progress should feel motivating.

Not stressful.

Users move forward through a journey—not a checklist.

---

# Video

Videos are part of the conversation.

Not external content.

The player should feel integrated into the session.

---

# Empty Space

Whitespace is a design element.

Never fill space simply because it exists.

---

# Icons

Simple.

Consistent.

Minimal.

Outlined whenever possible.

Never decorative.

---

# Images

Images should feel authentic.

Natural lighting.

Real people.

Real places.

No stock-photo feeling.

---

# Accessibility

Beauty should never reduce usability.

High contrast.

Keyboard support.

Clear focus states.

Comfortable font sizes.

Responsive layouts.

---

# Design Rule #1

Clarity before beauty.

---

# Design Rule #2

Beauty supports learning.

It never distracts from it.

---

# Design Rule #3

If removing an element improves focus, remove it.

---

# Design Rule #4

Every pixel should have a purpose.
